The Presidential Leadership Academy

A Culmination of my 3 year Experience

REFLECTION ON THE PLA
I was bound to be changed by my time at Penn State, but I never expected just how much I would be molded by my time in PLA. I can say confidently that it has changed me for the better, both in the way I think and the way I interact with the world around me and those living in it.
Coming into college, I was still very much the student that I was in high school. I was used to contributing often in class and making my presence known. But something changed after my freshman year and especially after joining the PLA. I fell in love with listening. Through all the coursework in PLA, I had the opportunity to hear both from my amazing peers and their thoughts as well as from distinguished faculty; proven leaders. It helped me to learn what I didn’t know.
You might think that this was a relatively safe course of action to be taking, and I’d say you’re right. But I didn’t take this position because I was afraid or unwilling to defend my own thoughts and positions. I just found it more valuable to spend time listening to others and let them help inform my own opinions, shaping them with nuance and perspective. I truly learned so much from everyone I ever heard from or interacted with in PLA.
That brings me to another takeaway from my time in PLA. The people you choose to surround yourself with, means everything. PLA surrounded me with some of the most incredible individuals at Penn State, and they made me into a better thinker and a better actor on my thoughts. They helped to open my mind and try to see the world differently. They also became an excellent network of friends and peers that would shape some of best moments in all my time here and inspire me to always seek more.
LEADERSHIP
My style of leadership tends to be leading from behind. By that I mean that never want to be forging ahead in a direction without an understanding of why I’m doing it, and without those I’m leading, at the very least, knowing about the decision. Ideally they should have input in my leadership decision and should feel that their input was respected and taken into account.
That certainly has always been the philosophy I’ve intended to practice in my leadership roles. What PLA has allowed me to do is to refine it. I’ve become a better listener, and found better ways to connect with those I’m leading. PLA has also helped me to see a bigger picture when leading, and to avoid leading with blinders on.
I've been lucky enough to hold leadership positions in two organizations on campus. On the Men's Club Volleyball Team I served as Travel Coordinator, Vice President, and President. Within THON I was a Technology Captain for THON 2020.

HONORS 201 & 401
201
A topic that we discussed in one semester of 401 with President Barron, that he felt strongly about, was the experience of students from minority populations at Penn State. I think this is both an incredibly important topic for the success of the university and one that is rarely discussed among students. Penn State is fairly homogenous in terms of its student population, but it should not cater to any one group; or rather not cater to a group because of its minority presence.
In a perfect world lectures would be focused around real experiences from real students at Penn State. Not necessarily students in class, as that could be uncomfortable or invasive, but if they were willing that would certainly be good. Talking about these issues relative to an incident can help bring the realness of this experience to those in class as well as focus the proposed changes that Penn State can make.
Should President Barron see it fit, this kind of a topic would also benefit greatly from brining in people that work at the PRCC and other groups on campus.
401
For this past semester of 401 (Spring 2020), I enjoyed the focus on student passions and interests. I felt it was a step in the right direction for the course moving forward. It was difficult to adapt during remote learning, but I think this syllabus allowed for the course to still bring tremendous value to those who took it.
While student led presentations were excellent, I feel that the content of the presentations should have an added focus to them. Everyone was obviously passionate about their topics and made excellent presentations, but it was sometimes difficult to discern where there might be anything worth debating that would help us grow as leaders. It certainly isn’t the case that disagreeing with someone or something is the only way we get better as leaders, but it can become easy to passively watch presentations without growing if we are simply enjoying someone else’s passion with them.
Perhaps these presentations could add a type of policy proposal. Not on the scale of that in 301, but enough that a tangible action is linked to someone’s passion. I think this could greatly increase discussion and interest in the course.
